How to Make Sourdough Bread

This is based on the Tartine Method and will make two loaves.

Ingredients

  • Strong white flour
  • Whole wheat flour
  • Sourdough starter
  • Water
  • Salt

Equipment

  • Large mixing bowl w/spoon
  • Medium mixing bowl for combining water
  • Large glass jar
  • Bench scraper
  • Proofing basket
  • Dutch oven
  • Kitchen scale
  • Thermometer

Day 1: Making the leaven

In the evening, gather the ingredients and equipment.

  1. Place glass jar on scale, set it to zero

  2. Add 100g of strong white flour

  3. Add 100g of whole wheat flour. If you don't reset the scale, the total should now be 200g.

  4. Add 200g of water. If you don't reset the scale, the total should be 400g.

  5. Add 1 tbsp of starter

  6. Mix well, cover with a lid, and place a rubber band on the jar as a marker.

  7. Let it sit overnight in a warm spot (approx. 78°F/26°C). The temperature will affect when the leaven is ready, so adjust accordingly

Day 2: Putting it all together

  1. In the morning, check to see if the leaven has doubled. Put it in a warm spot if it needs more time. You can begin the mixing process when it's ready (it should float).

  2. Place large mixing bowl on scale and set to zero

  3. Add between 600-800g of strong white flour

  4. Add remainder of whole wheat flour for a total of 1kg

  5. Place medium mixing container on scale and set to zero

  6. Add 600g of water

  7. Add 200g of leaven which should float. If you didn't reset the scale, the total should be 800g.

  8. Mix leaven and water together

  9. Add water/leaven mix to the flour

  10. Combine the mix, let it sit for 25-30 minutes at approx. 78°F (26°C)

After first rest period

  1. Measure 50g of water.

  2. Measure 20g of salt

  3. Add salt and water to the dough

  4. Mix thoroughly. Let it sit for another 25-30 minutes.

  5. Set some leaven aside as your new starter and refrigerate

Stretch and Fold

After the second rest period, stretch and fold the dough in the bowl. Do this every 30 minutes for approx. 3 hours. In between folds, cover with a damp towel. Maintain the dough temperature at around 78°F (26°C).

Shaping the dough

  1. When the stretching and folding is complete, place mixture out onto a floured surface

  2. Divide the dough in half

  3. Spread out the dough then fold from the sides

  4. Bring it together into a ball

  5. Gently form into a tight ball. Cover, let it sit for 30 minutes and repeat the shaping process (spreading, folding, shaping etc.)

  6. Place the dough seam side up in a floured proofing basket, cover and refrigerate overnight

Day 3: Baking

Preheat the oven to 500°F (260°C) with the Dutch oven inside for at least one hour.

  1. Dust the bottom of the loaf with corn flour

  2. Place the loaf in the Dutch oven, score the top

  3. Cover with lid and cook for 20 minutes

  4. After 20 minutes, remove the lid and lower the temperature to 400°F (200°C). Cook for another 20 minutes.

  5. Remove from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack

  6. Serve after a resting period (approx. 1-2 hrs.)
